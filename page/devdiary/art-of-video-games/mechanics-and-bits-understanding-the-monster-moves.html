Mechanics and Bits: Understanding the Monster Moves

It is undeniable that the 2nd Generation monsters were very “mechanical” so to say in terms of behaviour compared to their 3rd and 4th generation interaction. However there are a number of behaviours and monster mechanics that have been retained throughout the generation changes, with the monster aggression system being one of them. The monster aggression system in the newer generations mostly improves upon the existing move sets of the monster via improved hitboxes and incorporating verticality in their moves.

There are a number of moves and move categories that are shared between the monster types (such as the tail spin move for flying wyverns and Hipcheck of piscine wyverns), while the absence/presence of the moves emphasises upon not only the difficulty but also the signature of the monster. To make this statement clear we can compare two flying wyvern with two distinct move sets, the Rathian and Diablos.

Rathian is a flying wyvern that emphasises a lot more in terms of ranged attacks and status ailment. With moves such as the Fireball and Poisoned Tail Flip, Rathian focuses more on the ranged combat aspect while possessing only the basic close quarter moves that are shared among the flying wyvern such as Charge, Bite and Tail Spin. Diablos on the other hand focuses completely on the shock and awe with a complete absence of a ranged move set. Diablos features pure physical damage potential with a variety of distance closing moves such as the Horn Charge and Digging Charge, moves that are exclusive to itself and its relative Monoblos.

Move Categories

Close Frontal Attack: This is one of the most basic moves that every monster possesses regardless of its category. This move is manifested in the form of a simple frontal Bite or Claw Swipe. This move is usually done in a single direction with a very narrow width and can be avoided easily. The probability for this move to be executed is increased when the hunter is directly in front of the monster.

Close Area Control: This common move is used by monsters to hit a number of targets close to it. This move manifests most commonly in the form of Tail Spins of various Wyverns. Some monsters possess exclusive moves for this type of attacks such as the 360 Tail Spin of Tigrex or the Electrical Discharge of Khezu.

Ranged Attack: This move is usually elemental in nature and is manifested as a projectile, gas or secretion released by a monster. While the blights were introduced in the 3rd Generation to cause additional status effects, in the 2nd Generation this type of moves are elemental damage dealing ones only. There are however some exceptional ranged moves that instead cause physical damage such as the Rock Toss of Copper Blangonga or the Sand Spit of Cephadrome. Examples of elemental ranged moves include the Electric Ball attack of Khezu, Fireball of Rathian and Rathalos, Water Jet of Plesoith, and Ice Breath of Blangonga.

Distance Closing: This fast, aggressive move is used by the monster to shorten the distance between itself and the hunters. This type of move can be an attack in itself (Horn Charge of Monoblos and Diablos or the Charge of Congalala), or can be a move to shorten the gap between itself and the hunters for it to perform additional attack (Slithering attack of Piscine Wyverns or the Goofy Run of Gypceros). The probability of this move being executed is amplified when there is a considerable distance between the hunter and the monster itself.

Positional Manoeuvres: This type of moves usually is not intended to cause damage to the hunter, instead is used as a means to either create a distance between itself and the hunter (Flying Backwards move of Yian Kut Ku) or a way to protect itself (Shielding of Daimyo Hermitaur). This also includes moves that put the monster in an advantageous position such as Clinging to the Ceiling (by Khezu or Shogun Ceanataur) or Entering the Sand/ Water (Cephadrome/Plesioth). This move usually manifests in the form of a monster either flying or hopping backwards or upwards, or may also cause the monster to dig underground.
